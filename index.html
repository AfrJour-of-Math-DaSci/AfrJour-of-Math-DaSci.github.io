Aims & Scope

What we publish

The work we print has to be methodologically rigorous and societally relevant — across pure and applied math, statistics, theoretical computer science, optimization, ML and AI, scientific software, and data science. We take full research articles, short notes, surveys, software papers, and data papers. Every submission has to show a clear mathematical, statistical, algorithmic, or computational contribution. Purely descriptive applied work is out of scope.

We assess methodological correctness and the clarity of the contribution — not prospective citation impact, topical fashion, or fit with editor priors.

The journal runs bilingually: English is primary, French submissions are accepted with an extended English abstract.

Editorial Board

Founding members

The founding editorial committee will be announced in Q3 2026. The journal is led by an Editor-in-Chief, two Co-Editors covering the methodological and applied sides, and 15 to 20 Associate Editors organized by section, with an international Advisory Board sitting alongside.

The committee deliberately balances Anglophone, Francophone, and Lusophone Africa, the African diaspora, and international partners, with explicit gender-parity targets.

For authors

Submission

Submissions open in Q4 2026 via Open Journal Systems. We aim for a 16-week median from submission to first decision. Double-blind, two reviewers per manuscript.

At acceptance, authors deposit data and code in a citable open repository (Zenodo or institutional). Articles are published under CC BY 4.0; authors keep copyright. No fees for authors, no fees for readers.
